Engineering and Technology

Geographers studying engineering and technology study a wide variety of fields.

These include natural resources such as minerals, sustainability, wind, sunlight,

geothermal potential, pollution, etc. A geographer focused on these particular fields

would be interested in mapping the spatial relationships between these different features.

They would also examine how natural resources that can be exploited for energy may change

or exist based on the surrounding geographical features, and what particular locations may

be best to build structures based on the required resources. The relationship between

humans, resources, and spatial location is central to this area of study.
