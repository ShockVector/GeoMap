GIS & Remote Sensing

Perhaps the most widely applicable side of the study of Geography, GIS is the more technical side of the discipline involving the analysis and management of spatially referenced data for a huge variety of application areas. Careers in this field are abundant in both the public and private sector. Remote Sensing is a more concentrated aspect of GIS that deals with images of the ground or ground features that are captured via an aerial sensor. Careers in this field deal with the processing, interpretation, and use of imagery to supplement or conduct various studies or projects. Below are some examples of careers in these fields:

  • GIS Manager
  • GIS Analyst/GIS Technician
  • Programmer
  • Remote Sensing Technologist

Urban Planning & Development

Having to do with the design and development of the urban landscape, Geography is highly applicable to Urban Planning. GIS is primarily used to deal with urban land parcels in projects concerning land use, site suitability, legal issues, and more. However, other forms of geography may be used to assess the more humanistic issues of Urban Planning such as behavioral relationships and equity issues. Below is a list of careers within the Urban Planning and Development discipline area:

  • Infrastructure Management
  • Landscape Architect
  • Planner
  • Real Estate Appraiser
  • Surveyor

Environmental Management

Careers overlooking and managing the health of environments. Careers can fall public or private organizations. Public careers range from people managing our lands, forest, waters, and environments ensuring they are preforming at top shape. Additionally, Public organizations educated people on the environment and how to minimize human impact to preserve the environment. Most careers in the private section involve people making sure private companies are in compliance with environmental regulations.

  • BLM/Forestry
  • Environmental Health Manager
  • Nonprofit/Conservation Organization
  • PPGIS

Government/Business

Professions that analyze societies and social data for economical and governmental purposes. Governmental jobs range from a Natural Disaster Emergency Managers to people who analyze data on crime and social demographics to explain social trends of populations and to predict future trends. Economical jobs are people who look at the location and distribution of economic activities around the world to see how it impacts society.

  • Crime Analyst
  • Demographer
  • Emergency manager
  • GEOINT
  • OSHA
  • Policy Researcher

Academia/Research/Fieldwork

Those seeking an academic career in geography may be focused on becoming educators through positions such as university professor or high school teacher. Additionally, within the academic field there are many career opportunities in full-time research and fieldwork. Those hoping work in emerging fields, such as renewable energy, may also find their start within the realm of academic research. Geographers hold a unique opportunity for academic careers, as their specialized skills with spatial data and problem solving make them capable of interdisciplinary research associated with all field from science through the arts.

  • Contract Archaeologist
  • Professor
  • Renewable Energy

Internships

As a student or recent graduate, internships can provide some of the best experience for careers and often lead to full-time positions. Those seeking internships can find positions within their universities working alongside professors on research, or many geography students will find internships with companies that specialize in the areas of their own studies and career goals. Internships are listed here alongside careers as they require work and dedication, but students should recognize that many internships are unpaid and offer only experience. To locate an internship, students should review the many career fields that geography has to offer, then investigate and contact specific companies and/or professors to find positions.
